## 🔑 Three Core Non-Negotiables

### 1. Spiritual Core Must Be Authentic
✅ **DO**:
- Let readers feel "choir singing as body-spirit synchronized practice"
- Use concrete musical features (melisma, call-and-response, instrument sanctification)
- Ground spiritual thesis in actual music mechanics

❌ **DON'T**:
- Over-spiritualize (sounds like preaching)
- Over-romanticize (Gospel as "inspiration porn")
- Over-academicize (religious anthropology jargon blocks readers)

**Reference**: Unit 1-3 articles show how body transformation is written

---

### 2. Sister Rosetta Tharpe's Gender Identity Section — Nuanced Handling
✅ **DO**:
- Focus on tension between "church's sexual morality" vs "her spiritual freedom"
- Emphasize her as "queer pioneer" reshaping spiritual practice (not as social activist)
- Show how electric guitar, rock rhythm, bodily freedom = redefining Gospel itself

❌ **DON'T**:
- Write "LGBTQ+ inspirational story" (becomes identity politics)
- Over-emphasize church rejection (victim narrative)
- Ignore her musical achievements

**Key angle**: She challenged religion's *limits on spiritual practice*, not society's gender norms. That's the *spiritual freedom war*, not a social status war.

---

### 3. Taiwan Localization — Natural Integration
**Where**: H2-5 (social diagnosis) or H2-6 (contemporary music)

**Option A** (in H2-5):
- How do women & LGBTQ+ in Taiwan Presbyterian Church get music leadership?
- Shared "collective singing as spiritual practice" between Taiwan church music & Gospel
- Example: How Presbyterian women's hymn choirs gained church voice through singing

**Option B** (in H2-6):
- How do Taiwan contemporary spiritual musicians draw from Gospel?
- How they create Taiwan version of "body-spirit sync"?
- Example: Taiwan musicians blending Gospel elements with local culture

**Key**: Don't force a Taiwan paragraph. Show Taiwan case as *practicing Gospel's core*.

## ✋ Three Potential Traps

### Trap 1: Making It Too Academic
❌ "Gospel music emerged from African American folk traditions influenced by European hymnody..."
✅ "When Mahalia Jackson stepped to the mic, she didn't just sing—her whole body became an instrument of prayer."

### Trap 2: Identity Politics Over Spirituality
❌ "Sister Rosetta Tharpe was a groundbreaking LGBTQ+ figure..."
✅ "Tharpe's electric guitar was an act of spiritual rebellion: she was claiming the right to find God through her own body's freedom."

### Trap 3: No Taiwan Connection
❌ Adding a disconnected paragraph: "In Taiwan, there are also choirs..."
✅ Showing Taiwan case where Gospel's core principle (body-spirit sync through collective singing) is *lived* in actual Taiwan churches.
